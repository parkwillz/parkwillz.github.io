Introduction

I began my running journey my senior year of high school, I played basketball my entire life and decided to switch things up. My coach, Jessica Richards, took me under her wing and I started training with the high school team towards the end of my junior year. I joined the team to elevation train in Taos, New Mexico, where I immediately fell in love with the sport and I stuck with it through the summer training, and started with my first cross country season my senior year.

During high school cross country, I made it to regionals in my first year of running. I clocked a personal best in the 5k of 17:28. I then went right into track season, which got cancelled after a couple of meets due to COVID. In my short time in track, I ran personal bests of 2:09 in the 800m, and a 4:51 in the Mile. Despite COVID, I continued to train on my own, and during this time I ran a 10:31 on road in the 2 mile, and a 9:38 in the 3k.

COVID Training

When COVID hit I continued training with my friends and teammates. During this time I was running consistent mileage of around 45-50 miles per week, with my highest mileage week being 60 miles. I took a road trip to Colorado during this time to train at elevation, where we visited Colorado Springs and Rocky Mountain National Park.
